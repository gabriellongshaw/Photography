The Exposure Triangle

Every photograph is shaped by three settings working together: aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. Understanding how they relate is the key to full creative control.

Change one and you affect the others. A wide aperture lets in more light, so you can use a faster shutter or lower ISO. A slow shutter in dim light might require you to raise ISO. Mastering the balance separates snapshots from intentional images.

  • Aperture

    Controls how wide the lens opens. Affects both light and depth of field.

  • Shutter Speed

    Controls how long the shutter stays open. Affects motion and light.

  • ISO

    Controls sensor sensitivity. Higher values brighten the image but introduce noise.

Where to Start

If you're new to photography, start with aperture. It has the most visible effect on your images and is the foundation of understanding depth of field and exposure.
